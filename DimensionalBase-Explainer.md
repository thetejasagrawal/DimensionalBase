## Page 1: What's the Problem?

### Imagine two kids doing a group project...

You know how you can **picture** something really cool in your head — like a
dinosaur riding a skateboard — but when you try to **tell** your friend about
it, they imagine something totally different?

That's what happens with AI robots today.

```
  Robot A                          Robot B
  --------                         --------
  "I know the                      "Hmm, what does
   answer! Let me                    that even mean?
   write it down                     Let me guess..."
   in words..."

      Big beautiful thought
            |
            v
      Squished into words  -------->  Different thought
            (stuff gets lost!)
```

When Robot A learns something, it doesn't think in words. It thinks in
**numbers and shapes** — like a secret math language. But to talk to Robot B,
it has to squish everything into regular words. Robot B then has to **guess**
what those words meant. Every time this happens, important stuff gets lost.

It's like playing **telephone** — but worse, because robots do it thousands
of times a day.

### So what do we do?

What if the robots could just **share the shapes directly**?

No squishing. No guessing. Just: *here's exactly what I know.*

That's DimensionalBase.

## Page 2: How DimensionalBase Helps

### Think of it like a shared toy box

Instead of robots writing notes to each other and hoping for the best,
DimensionalBase gives them a **shared toy box** where they can put their
actual thoughts — the real shapes and numbers — not just words about them.

```
                    +---------------------------+
                    |                           |
                    |     THE SHARED TOY BOX    |
                    |     (DimensionalBase)     |
                    |                           |
                    |   Robot A puts in a toy   |
                    |   Robot B picks it up     |
                    |   It's the SAME toy!      |
                    |                           |
                    +---------------------------+
                       /                    \
                      /                      \
               Robot A                    Robot B
               "I put my                  "I got the
                thought in!"               EXACT thought!"
```

### It also does some really smart stuff:

| What it does | Like a 5-year-old would say... |
|---|---|
| **Remembers where things came from** | "Who said that first?" |
| **Knows who to trust** | "She's always right about dinosaurs, so I'll listen to her." |
| **Spots when robots disagree** | "Wait, you two said different things!" |
| **Packs a lunchbox perfectly** | "I only have a small bag — give me the MOST important stuff." |
| **Watches for news** | "Tell me if anything changes about the spaceship project!" |

### The 4 magic words

DimensionalBase is really simple. Robots only need **4 things**:

```
  PUT -----------> "Here, I learned something. Let me share it."

  GET -----------> "What do we know about this topic?"

  SUBSCRIBE -----> "Let me know if anything new happens."

  UNSUBSCRIBE ---> "OK, I'll stop watching now."
```

That's it! Four words. Even a robot-kindergartener could do it.

### Why should grown-ups care?

Because when AI robots can **actually understand each other** instead of just
passing notes back and forth, they can work together WAY better — like a
team that shares one brain instead of playing telephone.

DimensionalBase makes AI teamwork *actually* work.
